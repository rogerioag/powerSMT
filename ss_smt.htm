SS SMT - A Simulator for SMT Architectures

SS SMT is a simulator for SMT architectures that has been developed on the top of SimpleScalar Tool Set. The simulator is portable and provides many facilities to obtain detailed statistical information about the performance of SMT architecutres, under different configurations.

The SS SMT simulator was developed as a part of SEMPRE project, from APSE group. It was tested under GNU/Linux (x86) (Glibc 2.4/ Kernel 2.4) and in Solaris 2.7. People are invited to test it under other operational systems.

The SS SMT is maintained by Ronaldo A. L. Gonçalves (ronaldo@din.uem.br) from Universidade Estadual de Maringá (DIN/UEM) and by Guilherme Dal Pizzol (gpizzol@inf.ufrgs.br) from Federal University of Rio Grande do Sul (II/UFRGS).

More details on the simulator can be found here. The simulator source code/binary is available as a .tar.gz file. After downloading it, follow these instructions, that can also be found in the README file:

#tar xvfz ss_smt-1.0.tar.gz

#cd ss_smt

#make (you will need gcc)

#cd tests

#./run_it
